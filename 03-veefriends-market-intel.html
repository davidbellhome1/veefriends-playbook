VeeFriends Market Intelligence

What your cards are worth, where prices are heading, and what to sell first
Date: March 2026 · Focus: Spectacular 2026 Series · For: the Bell family (David, Patricia, Alex 15, Matthew 13) · 260 cards, $620 invested
⏰
Executive Summary: The VeeFriends collectible market is polarising — driven almost entirely by Gary Vaynerchuk's personal brand. The 2026 Spectacular Series is a sticker-based product (not traditional trading cards), released Feb 18, 2026 at $99/box. Prices for high-end pulls are strong ($200–$2,000+), but base/common stickers sell for under $1. Sell your rare pulls NOW while prices are at their post-release peak.

1. VeeFriends Product Lines — What Exists

2026 Super Stickers: Spectacular Series ⭐ (YOUR CARDS)

  • Released: February 18, 2026
  • Format: Stickers (not traditional trading cards), printed on card stock
  • Retail price: $99/box (+ $2.99 Run Fair fee), sold via raffle/pre-order
  • Configuration: 12 packs per box, 3 stickers per pack = 36 stickers per box
  • Set size: 100 characters (88 returning + 12 debut characters)
  • What's in a box: 2 Debut Stickers, 2 Numbered Parallels, 1 Spectacular Showdown insert, 1 Comic insert, 1 Mini Sticker (avg), 2 Scratch-off cards
  • Case: 12 boxes per case (~$1,157 average case price on eBay, trending DOWN ~$28 in past 30 days)

Base Stickers (88 characters)

  • 6 Spectacular foil finishes: Diamond, Lava, Bubble Gum, Hologram, Gold, Emerald
  • Each character in their Spectacular form on black backgrounds
  • GaryVee Autographs: Limited /2 (the 1/2 goes to NFT holder, 2/2 in packs — ULTRA rare)

Numbered Parallels (88 characters × 36 variations each)

  • Full-art backgrounds (character in Spectacular form + matching background)
  • 6 foil variations: Diamond, Lava, Bubble Gum, Hologram, Gold, Emerald
  • All individually serialized (first time ever for Super Stickers)
  • "Spectacular Match" = character form matches background type (the ultimate chase)

Characters: Arbitraging Admiral, Brave Bison, Conviction Cockroach, Cynical Cat, Daring Dragonfly, Gary Bee, Hot Shot Hornet, Juicy Jaguar, Knowing Gnome, Perfect Persian Cat, Respectful Racoon, Hard-Working Wombat

3. Topps Chrome 2025 Comparison & Trajectory

Understanding how Chrome performed tells us what to expect with Spectacular:

  • Release price: $99–$140 hobby box
  • Week 1–2 peak: Hobby boxes hit $500–$600+, singles commanding massive premiums
  • Month 2–3: Prices started settling, base cards became abundant
  • Month 6+ (Nov 2025): Hobby boxes still at $500–$600 (remarkably strong — Topps Chrome brand carrying it)
  • Current (Mar 2026, 10 months post-release): Likely $400–$500 range

Key Insight: Spectacular vs Chrome

Chrome benefited from: Topps brand recognition, Chrome finish (premium material), real celebrity autographs (Kevin Hart, Dana White, Michael Rubin, Daymond John), and the "first-ever" factor.

The Spectacular Series does NOT have these advantages — it's a sticker product, no celebrity autos, second Super Sticker product, lower perceived prestige.

📉
Conclusion: The Spectacular Series will likely follow a steeper decline curve than Chrome. Sell numbered/rare pulls NOW while the market is fresh.

4. Platform Price Comparison

eBay

  • Largest market for VeeFriends singles — 26,000+ active listings
  • eBay takes ~15% total fees (12.9% + processing ~3%)
  • Best for: individual high-value cards, numbered parallels, autographs
  • Authenticity Guarantee kicks in at $150+

Whatnot

  • VeeFriends has an official channel — @veefriends with active streams
  • Gary Vee himself has a Whatnot profile and streams regularly
  • Whatnot does $140,000 in VeeFriends revenue in a single 2-hour stream
  • Whatnot takes ~10% seller fee
  • Gary Vee streams create temporary price spikes

StockX

  • Lists sealed VeeFriends products (Chrome Hobby Box, Value Box, Sapphire)
  • Spectacular Series does NOT appear to be listed on StockX yet
  • StockX takes ~10% seller fee + ~3% payment processing
  • Best for sealed product only (not singles)

Arbitrage Opportunities

  1. Whatnot → eBay: Cards won cheaply in Whatnot live auctions can be listed higher on eBay as Buy It Now
  2. UK to US: UK sellers face less competition on eBay.com for VeeFriends; the market is almost entirely US-based
  3. Sealed Spectacular boxes at/below retail — NOT an arbitrage opportunity right now

  • No VeeFriends sellers streaming from the UK on Whatnot
  • The collecting community is overwhelmingly American

Opportunity

  • Lower shipping costs vs US sellers for European buyers
  • First-mover advantage in UK market

Risk

  • Buyer base for VeeFriends is 95%+ American
  • European collectors are a tiny fraction
  • Selling on eBay.com from the UK means slower shipping
  • Currency conversion eats into margins (GBP/USD)

Recommended Strategy

  1. List high-value cards on eBay.com (where the buyers are) — accept the fees and ship internationally
  2. List bundles/lots on eBay.co.uk to capture UK/EU market
  3. Consider Whatnot — UK seller streaming VeeFriends would be unusual enough to attract attention
  4. Ship from UK to US using tracked Royal Mail International (~£5–£7 for a card = competitive with US domestic)

Is Grading VeeFriends Worth It?

For Spectacular 2026 Stickers specifically: MOSTLY NO.

  1. These are stickers, not cards. Grading stickers is unusual and reduces the target buyer pool.
  2. Base stickers sell for $0.01–$2.00 raw. Adding $25–$50 in grading costs makes zero economic sense.
  3. The population is too new — no established pop reports means buyers don't yet know what's scarce.
  4. Stickers may not grade well — adhesive/edge issues can reduce grades.

When grading IS worth it:

  • GaryVee Autograph /2 — a $1,000+ card is worth the $50–$80 grading fee
  • 1/1 cards (Double Rainbow Debuts, Dual Autos) — authentication adds buyer confidence
  • Very low-numbered parallels (/5, /10) of popular characters if raw value exceeds $200+
  • Case hits (Diamond Die Cuts, 5 Year OG Art) if in pristine condition
📏
Rule of thumb: Only grade cards worth $100+ raw, and only if you're confident in a PSA 9 or 10. For a $100 raw card, a PSA 10 might push it to $200–$300 (2–3x), making the $35–$50 cost worthwhile.

9. Key Risk Factors

  1. Rapid depreciation: Modern non-sports releases typically lose 30–60% value within 6 months. The Spectacular product is likely past its peak already.
  2. Personality risk: VeeFriends' value is 100% tied to Gary Vaynerchuk. Any controversy, health issue, or shift in priorities could crater the market overnight.
  3. Market saturation: With ~5,900+ VeeFriends 2026 listings already on eBay (product only 3 weeks old), supply is outpacing demand for commons.
  4. Sticker format stigma: Traditional card collectors don't take sticker products as seriously as Chrome/refractor cards.
  5. NFT baggage: Many in the trading card community associate VeeFriends with failed NFT hype, creating resistance to the brand.
