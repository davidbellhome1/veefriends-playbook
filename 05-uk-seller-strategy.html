How to Package Trading Cards Safely

Standard packaging method (industry best practice):

  1. Penny sleeve — soft plastic sleeve to prevent scratching (cost: ~1p each in bulk)
  2. Toploader — rigid plastic holder that prevents bending (cost: ~5–10p each in bulk)
  3. Seal the toploader — piece of tape across the top opening (use painter's tape/washi tape, NOT sellotape which can damage cards)
  4. Team bag (optional but recommended) — resealable bag over the toploader, adds moisture protection (cost: ~2–3p each)
  5. Rigid envelope or bubble mailer — "Do Not Bend" cardboard-backed envelope or small bubble mailer

For higher-value cards (£20+):

  • Add thin cardboard on each side of the toploader
  • Use a bubble mailer instead of plain envelope
  • Consider semi-rigid card savers instead of toploaders (better for grading-bound cards)

When Is Tracking Worth It?

  • Under £10 card value: Untracked 2nd Class Large Letter is fine
  • £10–£30 card value: Consider Signed For or Evri (tracked), especially on eBay
  • Over £30 card value: Always tracked + signed for
  • Over £100: Royal Mail Special Delivery Guaranteed or tracked + insurance
  • On Whatnot: Whatnot provides prepaid shipping labels (handled for you)

USA-Specific Considerations (CRITICAL CHANGES)

🚨
US De Minimis Threshold SUSPENDED (since 29 August 2025): Previously, goods under $800 entered the US duty-free. This exemption has been suspended for ALL countries. Now ALL shipments to the US may be subject to duties and customs clearance. This adds extra fees and makes UK → US shipping less competitive vs US domestic sellers. The current US tariff environment includes a 10% universal tariff on most imports.

Customs Declarations

  • Description: "Trading cards" or "Collectible trading cards"
  • HS/Tariff Code: 9504.40.0000 (playing cards)
  • Country of origin: Where the cards were manufactured (often USA/Japan depending on brand)
  • Value: Declare the actual sale value — under-declaring is illegal and risky
  • CN22 for items under £270, CN23 for items over £270

VAT Considerations for UK Sellers

  • Exports are zero-rated for VAT — you don't charge UK VAT on items shipped outside the UK
  • Royal Mail international services are also zero-rated for VAT
  • If you're VAT-registered (threshold: £90,000 turnover), you reclaim input VAT but don't charge output VAT on exports
  • Most small card sellers won't hit the VAT threshold

Insurance for High-Value Cards

  • Royal Mail International Standard: compensation up to £20
  • Royal Mail International Tracked: compensation up to £50 (add £2.50 for up to £250)
  • For cards worth £250+: consider DHL Express or Parcelforce Global Express
  • Some sellers buy separate transit insurance through Parcel2Go

Better Options Than Royal Mail?

  • Evri International: From £6.08 for tracked, fully tracked to 200+ countries. Can be cheaper than Royal Mail for parcels.
  • DHL/FedEx/UPS: Much more expensive (£25–50+ for a single card) but faster and insured. Only for very high-value shipments.
  • Parcel2Go/ParcelMonkey: Comparison sites that find cheapest courier rates.
  • eBay Global Shipping Programme (GSP): Ship to a UK hub (Lichfield) and eBay handles all customs/international shipping. Removes all customs hassle from the seller.

Whatnot Shipping (How It Works for UK Sellers)

✅
On Whatnot, you don't need to worry about shipping costs as a seller. Whatnot partners with Royal Mail (domestic) and DPD (international). They provide prepaid shipping labels. Buyers pay shipping at checkout. You just need good packaging supplies. Multiple items from same buyer are bundled automatically.

3. Positioning as a UK Seller

Advantages

  1. Different timezone = less competition during UK streaming hours — Peak US Whatnot hours are 6pm–11pm EST (11pm–4am UK). Stream at UK evening hours with fewer live sellers competing.
  2. Access to UK-exclusive products — UK/European exclusive TCG printings, Pokémon Centre exclusives, UK/European football cards (Panini, Topps Match Attax)
  3. European buyers at cheaper shipping — UK to Europe is £3.80 for a Large Letter vs £4.60 to World zones. European collectors prefer buying from UK sellers.
  4. Cardmarket access — cardmarket.com is Europe's largest TCG marketplace. UK sellers can sell to European buyers with lower fees than eBay.
  5. VeeFriends UK gap — first-mover advantage — Very few (if any) UK-based VeeFriends sellers on Whatnot. A UK seller specialising in VeeFriends could attract UK/European buyers who currently pay expensive US shipping.

Disadvantages

  1. Higher shipping costs to US buyers — £4.60+ vs ~$1 for US domestic first class. With US de minimis suspension, buyers now face potential duties too.
  2. Smaller domestic market — UK trading card collector base is smaller than US. Fewer VeeFriends enthusiasts specifically.
  3. Sourcing challenges — Many TCG products launch in US first or are US-exclusive. Import costs for US-sourced inventory.
  4. Platform competition — Whatnot is still US-centric, though growing in UK. Most established sellers are American.

How to Compete

  • Focus on European market — position as "the EU/UK-friendly VeeFriends seller"
  • Bundle shipping — encourage buyers to purchase multiple cards per order
  • Include shipping in prices — instead of £5 card + £4.60 shipping, sell for £8 with "free international shipping"
  • Build a brand around being UK-based — it's memorable and differentiating
  • Stream at off-peak US hours — less competition, catch the European audience
  • Sell on multiple platforms — Whatnot for live sales, eBay UK for static listings, Cardmarket for European TCG buyers

4. The Boys' Involvement — Age Requirements

Whatnot Age Policy (VERY IMPORTANT)

⚠️
Official Whatnot rules for teens: You must be 18 or older to have your own account. Teens aged 13–17 can use Whatnot under the parent's account with supervision. An adult must appear at the beginning of each live stream. One Reddit user reported being banned after streaming at age 15 on their own account.

What This Means for the Bells

  • ✅ Alex (15) and Matthew (13) CAN appear on Whatnot streams
  • ✅ They can do the actual selling/presenting during live streams
  • ⚠️ The account MUST be in David's name
  • ⚠️ David must appear at the start of each stream (even briefly)

  • ❌ Neither boy can have their own seller account until they turn 18

Legal Considerations for Minors Selling Online in UK

  • No UK law prevents children from earning money — no minimum age for self-employment
  • Employment law restrictions don't apply to self-employment/family businesses the same way
  • Contract law: Minors (under 18) can enter contracts for "necessities" but other contracts may be voidable — hence platforms require adult account holders
  • The adult bears legal responsibility for transactions, consumer rights, and disputes

Tax Implications — Whose Income Is It?

  • If David provides the inventory and runs the account in his name — this is David's trading income
  • Special HMRC rule: if a parent "settles" income on minor children, and income exceeds £100 per year from each parent's settlement, it's taxed as the parent's income (ITTOIA/S629)
  • Safest approach: Treat all income as David's self-employment income initially. As the boys grow and take more ownership, this can evolve.

Safeguarding Considerations for Live Streaming

  • Don't reveal home address, school name, or other identifying details during streams
  • Be cautious about chat interactions — some viewers may be inappropriate
  • Whatnot has community guidelines against harassment, but live chat is hard to moderate
  • David should be present (or watching) during all streams with ability to end if needed
  • No child performance licence needed — this only applies to formal employment/entertainment work

5. Young Seller Success Stories

The "Family Selling" Angle

While specific UK teenage Whatnot sellers are rare, family-run card businesses are increasingly common:

  • Blake Martinez (ex-NFL player) turned Pokémon card selling on Whatnot into a $8.3M business in 9 months — started as a side hustle
  • Many successful Whatnot sellers started with small collections and grew
  • Reddit users report sellers making £300+ per stream on average, with top sellers doing £1,000–3,000 per week

The "Young Entrepreneur" Angle — Does It Help?

  • Viewers on Whatnot respond positively to enthusiastic young sellers who know their cards
  • The "family business" angle is heartwarming and memorable
  • On TikTok/Instagram, young card sellers get significant engagement
  • Warning: Don't lean too heavily on "cute kids" — card knowledge and genuine enthusiasm is what builds a lasting audience
  • Buyers want to feel they're supporting a genuine passion, not a gimmick

How Young Sellers Present Themselves

  • Focus on card knowledge — knowing set names, rarity tiers, card conditions, values
  • Energy and enthusiasm — young sellers often bring more energy than adults
  • Authenticity — don't try to be a corporate brand, be a family having fun with cards
  • Catchphrases and personality — successful Whatnot streamers have memorable styles
  • The boys being different ages brings natural dynamic (older/younger sibling energy)

Tips from Successful Family Accounts

  1. Have one person present and one manage chat/orders
  2. Create signature segments (e.g., "Mystery Monday" or "£1 Start Fridays")
  3. Let the personality shine — stiff corporate presentations don't work on Whatnot
  4. Engage with every single viewer, especially early on
  5. Run giveaways to build initial audience (no fees on Whatnot giveaways)

6. Business Setup & Tax

When Does Casual Selling Become a Business?

  • HMRC Trading Allowance: Earn up to £1,000 per tax year from trading income without telling HMRC
  • This is gross income (before expenses), not profit
  • If you're buying cards specifically to resell for profit, this is trading income from day one
  • Once you exceed £1,000 gross trading income → must register as self-employed

Platform Reporting to HMRC

📋
Since January 2024, digital platforms must report seller data to HMRC if you complete 30+ sales transactions in a calendar year, OR earn £1,707+ (~€2,000) in a calendar year. This applies to eBay, Whatnot, Vinted, Etsy, Amazon, Facebook Marketplace, etc.

Sole Trader Registration Process

  1. Register with HMRC as self-employed — do this by 5 October after the end of the tax year you started trading
  2. Go to gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment
  3. You'll need: National Insurance number, personal details, description of business
  4. You'll receive a Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) — takes about 10 days
  5. File a Self Assessment tax return by 31 January each year

Making Tax Digital (MTD) — Coming April 2026

  • From April 2026, sole traders with income over £50,000 must keep digital records using MTD-compatible software
  • Submit quarterly updates to HMRC
  • From April 2027, extends to those earning over £30,000
  • Below those thresholds, standard Self Assessment continues

Record Keeping Requirements

  • All sales — date, item, price, buyer, platform
  • All purchases (inventory) — what you bought, from whom, price, date
  • All expenses — packaging supplies, shipping costs, equipment, platform fees
  • Mileage — if driving to post office, card shows, etc.
  • Bank statements — separate business bank account is strongly recommended
  • Keep records for at least 5 years after the 31 January submission deadline

The VeeFriends UK Gap

🎯
This is a genuine first-mover opportunity. VeeFriends is US-centric with very few UK-based sellers on any platform. UK/European fans currently buy from US sellers and pay expensive shipping + potential import duties. A UK seller offering VeeFriends cards with cheap European shipping could carve out a niche. But: the UK market for VeeFriends specifically is small — this should be part of a broader TCG offering, not the only product line.

8. Family Action Plan

Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

David (Dad)

  • Register for a Whatnot seller account in your name — UK bank account, valid photo ID, selling description
  • Set up an eBay seller account (if not already done)
  • Order starter packaging supplies: 200 toploaders + penny sleeves (~£25), 100 team bags (~£5), 50 envelopes (~£8), 50 bubble mailers (~£8), painter's tape (~£3) — Total: ~£50
  • Consider buying a thermal label printer (Brother QL-800, ~£50–70)
  • Open a separate bank account for business transactions (Starling/Monzo = free)
  • Start a simple spreadsheet: inventory, purchases, sales, expenses

Alex & Matthew (The Boys)

  • Audit current VeeFriends card collection — catalogue every card with set, condition, estimated value
  • Research card values on eBay sold listings and Whatnot recent sales
  • Watch successful Whatnot card sellers (study style, pacing, engagement)
  • Practice presenting cards on camera — film each other on phones
  • Each pick a role: one presenter, one chat manager (can rotate)

Family

  • Agree on a brand name — check availability on Whatnot, eBay, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube
  • Create a simple logo (Canva is free)
  • Set up streaming space: clean desk, good lighting (ring light ~£15), phone stand, neutral background
  • Attend the nearest upcoming card show together

Phase 2: Launch (Weeks 4–8)

  • List 20–30 cards on eBay with good photos and descriptions — learn the listing process
  • Schedule and run first Whatnot live stream (30–60 minutes)
  • David appears at the beginning, then boys take over
  • Mix of auction items and "Buy Now" items — consider starting low (£1–2) to attract bidding
  • Prepare 1–2 giveaway items to attract viewers
  • Create TikTok/Instagram accounts, post first content
  • Ship ALL orders within 24 hours — fast shipping = good reviews = more sales

Phase 3: Grow (Months 2–6)

  • Increase stream frequency (2–3 per week)
  • Expand beyond VeeFriends — Pokémon is massive on Whatnot
  • Source inventory: eBay bulk lots, card shows, Cardmarket, wholesale contacts
  • Experiment with stream formats: mystery packs, claim sales, themed streams
  • Build European shipping angle — actively promote to European buyers
  • Track finances — when approaching £1,000 gross income, prepare for HMRC registration

Phase 4: Establish (Months 6–12)

  • Register as sole trader with HMRC (if not already done)
  • Consider VAT-awareness (still far from £90k threshold, but plan ahead)
  • Evaluate MTD software needs if income grows
  • Potentially take a vendor table at a card show
  • Explore Cardmarket for European TCG sales
  • Build the YouTube channel — weekly content minimum
  • Review: fun side hustle or serious business? Decide direction together.
