How to Get Set Up (Step by Step) ▼

Application Process

  • Create account at whatnot.com in David's name (18+ required)
  • Apply at whatnot.com/seller — choose "Trading Card Games" category
  • Need: UK passport, proof of address, links to any existing eBay shop or social media
  • Record a 30-60 second video showing your face, your cards, and your selling style
  • Approval typically takes 5-10 business days
  • After approval: complete Stripe Connect for GBP payouts to UK bank account
  • Attend the mandatory Zoom onboarding session (brief quiz at the end)

Teen Seller Rules

  • Account MUST be in parent's name — David is legally responsible for everything
  • Both boys (13+) can use the account under supervision
  • An adult must appear at the beginning of each live stream
  • David's ID, bank details, and legal identity are on the account
  • ⚠️ Teens who create their own accounts risk permanent bans

How Live Selling Actually Works ▼

The Format

  • You go live, show cards one at a time, and viewers bid in real-time
  • Auctions run 15 seconds to 2 minutes — fast-paced and exciting
  • Highest bidder wins and is charged automatically through the app
  • You can also have Buy-It-Now items available alongside auctions
  • Giveaways are essential for building initial audience
  • Chat interaction is key — talk to your viewers, answer questions, have fun

How Whatnot Handles Shipping (UK Sellers)

  • Whatnot provides prepaid shipping labels — you don't pay for shipping
  • Buyers pay shipping at checkout (calculated by weight/distance)
  • Domestic UK: via Royal Mail. International: via DPD
  • Multiple items from same buyer are bundled automatically ("Smart Bundling")
  • You just need good packaging supplies — everything else is handled

The Algorithm — How to Get Seen

  • Consistency is #1 — stream at the same times every week
  • Miss a week and your viewership tanks. Change schedule? Start over.
  • Follower engagement matters — the more people who turn on notifications, the better
  • New sellers get a brief "new seller boost" — use it wisely
  • High ratings and fast shipping improve your visibility

Tactics That Actually Work ▼

$1 Starts — The Whatnot Standard

$1 starting bids are the norm. They attract more viewers and bidders. FOMO and competitive bidding can push prices above market value. But items CAN sell for $1 if viewership is low.

  • Use $1 starts on items you can afford to lose on (loss leaders)
  • Start higher ($5-$25) on valuable items until you build an audience
  • Use BIN listings for items where you need a specific price

Giveaways — Essential for Growth

  • Run giveaways during streams to attract new followers
  • Keep them small — a decent card, not a PS5
  • Reward loyal viewers to keep them coming back
  • Cost: ~78p per giveaway (shipping fee on Whatnot)

Consistency is Everything

"Consistency is the #1 factor in a new seller's success" — Whatnot staff. Stream at the same times every week. Top sellers do 3-6 nights per week, 3-5 hours per session.

Community Building

  • "Raid trains" — send viewers to another seller's show when yours ends
  • Cross-promote on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube
  • Build a Discord server for your community
  • Engage with every single viewer, especially when small

Common Mistakes & Pitfalls ▼
  • Burnout — Performing on camera 12-16 hours weekly is exhausting. Many love it for 3-6 months, then dread shows
  • $1 starts going wrong — Selling a £50 card for £1 with low viewership
  • Sourcing problems — Running out of inventory to sell
  • Oversaturation — Too many sellers in popular categories
  • Algorithm dependency — Miss a week and viewership tanks
  • Non-paying bidders — New accounts win auctions and never pay
  • Platform risk — Whatnot can ban sellers with little recourse. Policy changes can destroy business models overnight
  • Cash flow — Until 1,000 completed deliveries, payouts are delayed 2-3 days

UK Advantages & Positioning ▼

Why Being UK-Based Helps

  • Different timezone — stream at UK evening hours with less competition from US sellers
  • Cheaper European shipping — £3.80 to Europe vs £4.60 worldwide. European buyers prefer UK sellers.
  • VeeFriends UK gap — virtually zero UK-based VeeFriends sellers. First-mover advantage.
  • Access to UK-exclusive products — Pokémon Centre UK exclusives, UK football cards
  • Cardmarket access — Europe's largest TCG marketplace, not easily accessible from US

How to Compete

  • Position as the "EU/UK-friendly VeeFriends seller"
  • Bundle shipping — encourage multiple card purchases per order
  • Include shipping in prices (£8 with "free shipping" beats £5 + £4.60 postage)
  • Stream at off-peak US hours — less competition, catch European audience
  • Sell on multiple platforms simultaneously

Family Action Plan
The exact steps to go from where you are now to selling cards online

Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

Get set up, get organised, learn the platforms.

  • David: Apply for Whatnot seller account (in your name). Set up eBay seller account.
  • David: Order packaging supplies: 200 toploaders + sleeves (~£25), 100 team bags (~£5), 50 envelopes (~£8), bubble mailers (~£8) = ~£50 total
  • David: Open a separate bank account for card business (Starling/Monzo = free and instant)
  • Alex & Matthew: Catalogue every card — set, condition, estimated value
  • Alex & Matthew: Research values on eBay sold listings. Watch successful Whatnot streamers.
  • Alex & Matthew: Practice presenting cards on camera — film each other on phones
  • Family: Agree on a brand name. Check availability on Whatnot, eBay, Instagram, TikTok.
  • Family: Set up streaming space: ring light (~£20), phone stand (~£12), clean desk
  • Family: Join VeeFriends Discord (270K members, active trading channels)

Phase 2: Launch (Weeks 4–8)

Start listing, run your first stream, learn by doing.

  • List 20-30 best cards on eBay — good photos, market-driven prices
  • Schedule and run first Whatnot live stream (30-60 minutes)
  • David appears at the start of stream, then boys take over
  • Mix of auction items ($1 starts on cheaper cards) and BIN items
  • Run a giveaway to attract first viewers
  • Create TikTok/Instagram, post first unboxing or card showcase content
  • Ship ALL orders within 24 hours — fast shipping = good reviews = more sales
  • List bundles of base stickers on eBay (individual bases aren't worth listing)

Phase 3: Grow (Months 2–6)

Build consistency, expand inventory, diversify.

  • Increase stream frequency to 2-3 per week
  • Consider expanding beyond VeeFriends — Pokémon is massive on Whatnot
  • Source inventory: eBay bulk lots, card shows, Cardmarket, local sellers
  • Attend a UK card show together (London Card Show XL in April!)
  • Experiment with formats: mystery packs, claim sales, themed streams
  • When approaching £1,000 gross income, prepare for HMRC registration

Phase 4: Establish (Months 6–12)

Solidify the business, scale what works.

  • Register as sole trader with HMRC (if income exceeds £1,000)
  • Consider a vendor table at a card show
  • Build YouTube channel alongside Whatnot/TikTok
  • Evaluate: fun side hustle or serious business? Decide direction as a family.
  • Watch for Fanatics Live UK launch — get in early
🎯
The "sell now" priority: List your numbered parallels, autographs, and case hit inserts on eBay immediately. VeeFriends prices decline over time — your rarest cards are worth the most right now. Base stickers should be bundled (lots of 10-20) or sold in bulk on Whatnot streams.
